How it works

Three simple parts

1  Tag it

Stick a small battery-powered tag on anything you want to follow — forklifts, carts, pallets. About $18 each, and the battery lasts years.

2  Sensors listen

Inexpensive sensors on the ceiling pick up each tag's signal. They just listen — nothing to wire into your existing systems.

3  Watch the map

Software turns those signals into a moving dot on your floor plan. Open it on any screen and watch the floor in real time.

It's basically indoor GPS. When three sensors hear the same tag, that's enough to pin it on the map — the same trick your phone's GPS uses, just built for inside a building.

Why it's worth it

See exactly how long things sit in each area. No clipboards, no stopwatches, no guessing where the holdups are.

Cut the wasted steps

The map shows every unnecessary trip across the floor. Trim the back-and-forth and you trim the labour cost with it.

The honest part

It's not magic — here's the catch

Cheap and useful, but you should know its limits before you count on it.

Close, not pinpoint

It's accurate to within a few metres — perfect for "which area is it in, and how long was it there," not "which exact shelf." For workflow and bottlenecks, that's plenty.

Big metal blocks signals

Tall steel racking gets in the way, so dense areas need a few more sensors. Worth a quick walk-through before you decide how many to buy.

Battery vs. speed

Tags can update faster or last longer, but not both at once. Fast updates for busy forklifts, slow-and-steady for things that rarely move.
