HABIT TRACKER · How it works

Three sliders. One face. A few seconds.

A check-in isn't a journal entry. Three sliders for mood/focus/energy, a drag for sleep, toggles for the things you're watching.

Built to be changed.

Nothing in either app is wired to anyone's specific life — categories, labels, colours, the things being tracked. The structure is opinionated about the shape; the contents are yours.

PILLAR 01
No accounts, no cloud.
Install on a phone. Data lives in the phone and in your own email. Nothing logs in anywhere.
PILLAR 02
Works offline.
Service worker means it opens on a dead-zone subway. Submissions wait in mail until service comes back.
PILLAR 03
Edits are words.
Categories and labels sit in config blocks at the top of one HTML file. Find. Replace. Save.
TIP Let an AI rewrite it for your life. You don't need to code. Drop the HTML into a Claude or ChatGPT chat with a short description of what you want — categories, labels, colours, what you're tracking — and ask for an updated file back.
DASHBOARD A place for the data to land. A separate desktop app is in the works to read the master Excel files these trackers feed into — charts, monthly views, trend lines. Keeps the phone apps simple and pushes the analysis off the phone. ↗ Dashboard app (coming soon)
AI PROMPT EXAMPLE

Something you can paste straight in.

Here's the kind of message that gets useful results. Specific, short, and tells the AI what to give back.

"I've attached two HTML files for a spending tracker and a habit tracker. I want to use these for myself. For the spending tracker, change the categories to: groceries, gas, kids' activities, eating out, subscriptions, savings, and 'fun money'. Use a soft green colour theme. For the habit tracker, change the sliders to mood, anxiety, and motivation — drop the energy slider. Change the toggles to: walked the dog, took meds, read 10 pages, no phone after 9pm. Send me back both updated HTML files."

The clearer you are about what's in and what's out, the cleaner the result. You can iterate — ask for tweaks, ask for screenshots of what changed.
